# Using Mirror in Chrome

Short guide for **people using** the Mirror extension—not engineers.

## In one sentence

Mirror sits in Chrome’s side panel while you shop: it **shows clothes on you** with AI try-on, helps you **compare price and resale**, and gives you a place to **save items**, **share fits**, and **see what applies to the page you’re on**—using the **same login** as the Mirror website.

## How it flows

1. **Sign in** — Use Google, Apple, or email (use the **same email** as the Mirror web app if you use both). The first time, you’ll add your reference photo **in the web app** so try-on knows what you look like.

2. **Home** — Mirror reads **the tab you’re shopping in** and shows what it found: the item’s picture, save options, and quick tiles for price, try-on, asking friends, and fit score (some tiles fill in after you try something on). If you’re not on a product page, it may show little or wrong context—switch to the store tab or use the **M** control on the edge of the page to refresh.

3. **Try on me** — Opens **Try-on**. You pick **top**, **bottom**, or focus on **you**, choose a piece from **things you saved** or **this page**, then tap **Generate** to see the AI result.

4. **Share** — After a try-on, you can share it to **your feed** so it appears in Mirror’s social strip.

5. **Circle** — On a product page, this is where **friend-style feedback** for *that item* will show once you’ve posted about it here; until then you’ll see hints and empty states.

6. **Feed** — Your **fit feed**: posts you shared, with reactions and comments when people engage.

**Bottom line:** sign in → shop on a product page → try on → optionally share → check Circle for that product or browse your feed.

## What you’ll see (in order)

Walkthrough matches opening Mirror from scratch through your first posts.

1. **Welcome & sign-in** — Mirror branding, a short line about social try-on with Phia, big buttons for **Google** and **Apple**, or **email and password**, plus **Stay signed in** and **Sign in**. New users can **create an account**.

2. **Home** — The item Mirror thinks you’re looking at (on a store tab that’s the **big picture** on the left). You can **bookmark** to your closet, tap **I own this** if you already have it, and see tiles for **price**, **try-on readiness**, **circle**, and **fit score**. There’s also text if Mirror needs a clearer product page. Big actions: **Try on me** and **Ask circle**.

3. **Try-on** — A large preview of **you**, a **Generate** button, and three chips: **Avatar**, **Top**, **Bottom** (pick what you’re changing). Under that: **Choose garment** and where to pull from (saved vs this page). The bar at the bottom switches **Home**, **Try-on**, **Circle**, **Feed**.

4. **Choosing a garment** — Filters like **Tops**, **Bottoms**, and **This page**; a list of items with a small photo, title, and brand (for example a shirt from lululemon). **This page** may be grayed out when Mirror doesn’t have a garment from the current tab.

5. **Circle** — Title **What your circle thinks**, which site or page you’re on, a **Fit score** area that’s coming soon, and a dashed box when nothing is linked to this product yet—with a tip to try on and share first.

6. **Feed** — **The fit feed** with a **Live** tag, cards with **who posted**, **when**, the try-on photo, a **Try-on** label, reaction and comment counts, and a short **Fit check** caption.
